Fake News

What is Fake News?

Fake news refers to false or misleading information presented as news, often with the intention to deceive or manipulate readers. It spreads rapidly on social media and other platforms, blurring the lines between fact and fiction.

In a country, fake news can cause widespread misinformation, influence public opinion unfairly, incite social unrest, damage reputations, and even interfere with elections or policy decisions. It threatens the credibility of legitimate media and can deeply affect national unity, safety, and democracy.





Why It Matters

Importance of Fake News Detection

In any country, the spread of fake news can create confusion, fear, and mistrust among people. It can mislead citizens during elections, trigger social conflicts, and harm the reputation of individuals or communities.

Detecting fake news is critical to ensure that people receive reliable and accurate information. By identifying and filtering out false content, we can protect national security, support democratic processes, and promote social harmony. In today’s digital era, where information spreads instantly, fake news detection plays a vital role in maintaining truth and transparency.
