Fake News Detection in Country

Statistical Analysis


Detecting Fake News through Data

In today's digital age, fake news has become a serious concern, influencing public opinion and critical decision-making across politics, health, and finance. This study focuses on applying statistical and visualization techniques to identify patterns in fake and real news articles. Using datasets such as Bangla News and LIAR, we explore linguistic trends, content structures, and correlations that help distinguish real news from misinformation.

Through methods like word count analysis, text length distribution, correlation heatmaps, and entity recognition, we aim to uncover the underlying statistical traits of fake news. These insights are crucial for building more accurate and reliable automated fake news detection systems.

Implemented Modules


Data Preprocessing

Cleaning of text data by removing duplicates, stopwords, and punctuation. Computation of word, character, and sentence counts.


Category Distribution

Bar chart visualization of the frequency of different news categories (e.g., Politics, Sports, etc.).


Fake vs. Real News Distribution

Pie chart and bar plot to visualize the class imbalance between real and fake news articles.


Word & Text Length Analysis

Histogram and box plot visualizations showing differences in word and character counts between fake and real news.


Correlation Heatmap

A heatmap to analyze correlations between text features like word count, character count, and sentence count.


Speaker & Source Analysis

Visualization of frequent statement originators in the LIAR dataset to analyze the role of individuals in misinformation.


Emotion Analysis

Sentiment distribution of articles into positive, negative, and neutral to evaluate emotional tones in fake vs. real news.


Named Entity Recognition

Identification of top recurring names and organizations in fake and real news to detect entity-level bias.


Word & Text Length Analysis


Comparing Fake and Real News Structures

This module focuses on analyzing the structure of fake and real news articles using word and character count distributions. Histograms and box plots are employed to visualize text lengths across both categories.

Findings indicate that real news articles tend to be longer than fake ones. This structural difference helps in understanding writing styles and summarization patterns, which can be vital in feature engineering for classification models.

Fake vs Real News Distribution


Class Imbalance Insights

A class imbalance was observed, where real news significantly outnumbers fake news. Pie charts and bar plots were used to visually present this imbalance.

This observation is crucial for building machine learning models, as imbalanced datasets can lead to biased predictions. Understanding the ratio of fake to real helps in selecting appropriate resampling techniques.

Category Distribution


Topic Prevalence in News

A bar chart visualization showed the frequency of various categories like National, Sports, Politics, and more in the dataset. It provides a quick glance into which topics are most covered.

This helps in understanding where fake news is more likely to appear, and whether certain topics are more vulnerable to misinformation.

Word & Text Length Analysis


Comparing Writing Styles

Histograms and KDE plots were created to compare word and character count distributions across fake and real news. Real news articles were found to be generally longer.

This structural insight helps in identifying verbosity patterns and writing habits commonly associated with misinformation.

Correlation Heatmap


Exploring Relationships in Features

The correlation heatmap highlighted strong relationships between features like word count, character count, and sentence count.

This helped in feature selection and understanding which attributes could contribute most to machine learning models for classification.

Speaker & Source Analysis


Who Spreads the Fake News?

Analysis of the LIAR dataset revealed key individuals and media sources contributing significantly to false or misleading claims.

This helps track misinformation sources and offers deeper insights into credibility and media responsibility.

Emotion Analysis


Sentiment Behind the Words

Articles were categorized based on emotional tone (positive, neutral, or negative). Fake news often carried more emotionally charged content.

Emotion analysis plays a key role in understanding how fake news manipulates reader perception and engagement.

Named Entity Recognition


Identifying Key People & Places

The system extracted top entities (names, places, organizations) mentioned in both fake and real news articles.

This analysis revealed how fake news often centers around specific figures or themes, helping uncover bias and repetition in misinformation.
